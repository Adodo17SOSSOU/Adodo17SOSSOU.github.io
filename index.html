Brief academic bio

I am a machine learning engineer and researcher interested in deep learning, spatio-temporal models and medical imaging. I am currently completing the Master’s programme Mathematics, Vision, Learning (MVA) at ENS Paris-Saclay / Université Paris Cité with distinction.

During my Master’s thesis at Télécom Paris (Institut Polytechnique de Paris), I worked on Alzheimer’s disease progression modeling, combining self-supervised 3D MRI encoders with probabilistic longitudinal models. Previously, I studied Computer Science at Université Sorbonne Paris Nord, after an engineering cycle and intensive preparatory classes in mathematics, computer science and physics in Morocco.

I have been supported by several competitive scholarships, including the Paris Artificial Intelligence Research Institute Excellence Scholarship and a Graduate School Excellence Scholarship.

Current work & research interests

Broadly, I am interested in using machine learning to improve healthcare and neuroscience, with a focus on:

  • Deep learning for 3D medical imaging
  • Spatio-temporal and probabilistic modeling of disease progression
  • Self-supervised learning and representation learning
  • Robust and reliable AI systems in health applications

My Master’s thesis develops a pipeline for disease trajectory inference, risk prediction and normative scoring using brain MRI, with an emphasis on reproducible research (Python/PyTorch, Slurm, bootstrap confidence intervals and explainability analyses).

Selected projects

A few recent technical projects (see my GitHub for details):

  • Accented English ASR benchmark – complete evaluation pipeline for Whisper and Wav2Vec 2.0 on French-accented English (Mozilla Common Voice), fully automated with Python, PyTorch, Hugging Face and Slurm. Achieved WER 0.218 vs 0.328 baseline.
  • Medical domain ASR – benchmarking ASR models on radiology, pathology and surgery datasets, reaching near-zero WER with Whisper in specialized contexts.
  • Time-series systems & streaming analysis – real-time anomaly detection and forecasting pipeline (Z-score, ARIMA) with automated data generation and visualization.

Study abroad journey

My study abroad path started in Togo, continued through Morocco and has brought me to France. I completed two years of intensive preparatory classes and an engineering cycle at ENSA Fès, with a fully funded scholarship from the Togolese government and AMCI, before moving to Paris for my Master’s studies.

Based on this experience, I founded Excellence Scholars, an initiative to help students from around the world access scholarships and high-quality educational opportunities. The community now includes thousands of students across social platforms and messaging groups.

Through both my own trajectory and this project, I aim to support others in navigating applications, funding opportunities and transitions between countries and systems.
